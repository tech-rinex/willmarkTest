Projects under Ansys

Aerodynamics of Golf Ball

In this project, the projection of a golf ball is studied aerodynamically at a speed with lateral angle and zero degree angle of attack, using ANSYS Fluent CFD Simulation. In this dynamic simulation, the aerodynamic coefficients of quasi-experimental software and coefficients calibrated with Computational Fluid Dynamics are used.

Stress Analysis of Engine Camshaft

Finite Element is used for the modeling and static structural analysis of the engine camshaft. The camshaft is a part of the internal combustion (IC) engine that moves at great speeds causing stress and vibrations in the automobile system. The main concern and goal of the project is to predict the service life of the camshaft and extend it.

Airflow Analysis inside Oil Tanker

Oil/Fuel storage and transportation tanks are prone explosive during times of high environmental temperatures. In order to avoid this, the tanks are facilitated with air vents to prevent the evaporated fuel from accumulating inside the chambers during the times of storage. This project uses compute aided design tools to analyse the airflow patterns and design the tanker conveniently.
